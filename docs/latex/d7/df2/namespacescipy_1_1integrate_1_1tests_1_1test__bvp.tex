\begin{DoxyVerb} Big version of sl_fun, with two parameters.

The two differential equations represented by sl_fun are broadcast to the
number of rows of y, rotating between the parameters p[0] and p[1].
Here are the differential equations:

    dy[0]/dt = y[1]
    dy[1]/dt = -p[0]**2 * y[0]
    dy[2]/dt = y[3]
    dy[3]/dt = -p[1]**2 * y[2]
    dy[4]/dt = y[5]
    dy[5]/dt = -p[0]**2 * y[4]
    dy[6]/dt = y[7]
    dy[7]/dt = -p[1]**2 * y[6]
    .
    .
    .
\end{DoxyVerb}
