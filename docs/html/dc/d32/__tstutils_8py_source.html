1r"""
2Parameters used in test and benchmark methods.
3
4Collections of test cases suitable for testing 1-D root-finders
5 'original': The original benchmarking functions.
6 Real-valued functions of real-valued inputs on an interval
7 with a zero.
8 f1, .., f3 are continuous and infinitely differentiable
9 f4 has a left- and right- discontinuity at the root
10 f5 has a root at 1 replacing a 1st order pole
11 f6 is randomly positive on one side of the root,
12 randomly negative on the other.
13 f4 - f6 are not continuous at the root.
14
15 'aps': The test problems in the 1995 paper
16 TOMS "Algorithm 748: Enclosing Zeros of Continuous Functions"
17 by Alefeld, Potra and Shi. Real-valued functions of
18 real-valued inputs on an interval with a zero.
19 Suitable for methods which start with an enclosing interval, and
20 derivatives up to 2nd order.
21
22 'complex': Some complex-valued functions of complex-valued inputs.
23 No enclosing bracket is provided.
24 Suitable for methods which use one or more starting values, and
25 derivatives up to 2nd order.
26
27 The test cases are provided as a list of dictionaries. The dictionary
28 keys will be a subset of:
29 ["f", "fprime", "fprime2", "args", "bracket", "smoothness",
30 "a", "b", "x0", "x1", "root", "ID"]

119# f6(x) returns random value. Without memoization, calling twice with the
120# same x returns different values, hence a "random value", not a
121# "function with random values"
122_f6_cache = {}
123def f6(x):
124 v = _f6_cache.get(x, None)
125 if v is None:
126 if x > 1:
127 v = random()
128 elif x < 1:
129 v = -random()
130 else:
131 v = 0
132 _f6_cache[x] = v

642def get_tests(collection='original', smoothness=None):
643 r"""Return the requested collection of test cases, as an array of dicts with subset-specific keys
644
645 Allowed values of collection:
646 'original': The original benchmarking functions.
647 Real-valued functions of real-valued inputs on an interval with a zero.
648 f1, .., f3 are continuous and infinitely differentiable
649 f4 has a single discontinuity at the root
650 f5 has a root at 1 replacing a 1st order pole
651 f6 is randomly positive on one side of the root, randomly negative on the other
652 'aps': The test problems in the TOMS "Algorithm 748: Enclosing Zeros of Continuous Functions"
653 paper by Alefeld, Potra and Shi. Real-valued functions of
654 real-valued inputs on an interval with a zero.
655 Suitable for methods which start with an enclosing interval, and
656 derivatives up to 2nd order.

671 if smoothness is not None:
672 tests = [tc for tc in tests if tc['smoothness'] >= smoothness]
